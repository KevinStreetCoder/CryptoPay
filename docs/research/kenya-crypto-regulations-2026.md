# Kenya Cryptocurrency Regulatory Landscape (March 2026)

## 1. CBK (Central Bank of Kenya) Stance

**Historical (2015-2023):** Hostile — warnings, banks discouraged from servicing crypto.

**Major shift (2024-2025):** National Treasury directed creation of regulatory framework. Multi-agency Technical Working Group formed. Draft National Policy on VAs & VASPs published December 2024.

**Current (2026):** CBK is co-regulator under VASP Act 2025. Primary jurisdiction over:
- Virtual Asset Wallet Providers (custodial)
- Virtual Asset Payment Processors
- Stablecoin issuers

Licensing has NOT yet started — implementing regulations still being drafted.

## 2. CMA (Capital Markets Authority) Licensing

Under VASP Act 2025, CMA oversees:
- Virtual Asset Exchanges
- Digital Asset Brokers
- Investment Advisors (virtual assets)
- Fund Managers
- Tokenization platforms

**Licensing is mandatory** but not yet commenced. Waiting for Cabinet Secretary to issue implementing regulations.

## 3. VASP Act 2025 (Act No. 20 of 2025)

- Assented by President Ruto: **October 15, 2025**
- Commenced: **November 4, 2025**

**Key requirements:**
- Only Kenyan-incorporated companies (or foreign with Kenyan compliance cert)
- Kenyan bank account mandatory
- Minimum paid-up share capital and liquidity buffers (amounts TBD)
- Kenyan nationals on the board
- Physical office in Kenya
- Cybersecurity requirements (standards TBD)
- **12-month grace period** — existing operators until ~November 2026

**Regulated activities:** Exchanges, custodial wallets, brokerages, payment gateways, stablecoin issuance, token issuance, virtual asset transfer services.

## 4. AML/KYC Requirements

VASPs designated as **Reporting Institutions** under POCAMLA:
- Mandatory KYC and Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
- Transaction monitoring systems
- Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) to Financial Reporting Centre (FRC)
- Record-keeping obligations
- Periodic risk assessments
- On-site/off-site regulatory inspections
- FATF Travel Rule implementation

## 5. Tax Implications

| Period | Tax | Rate | Basis |
|--------|-----|------|-------|
| 2023 (Finance Act 2023) | Digital Asset Tax | 3% | Gross transaction value |
| July 2025+ (Finance Act 2025) | **Excise Duty** | **10%** | **Service fees charged by VASPs** |

- 3% DAT was **repealed** by Finance Act 2025
- Replaced with 10% excise duty on VASP fees (NOT transaction value — massive reduction)
- VASPs collect and remit to KRA by 20th of following month
- Capital Gains Tax (5%) on crypto appreciation — user's responsibility
- Income tax applies if crypto received as payment for services

## 6. Legal Feasibility: Crypto-to-M-Pesa Service

**Yes, legally viable with conditions:**
1. VASP license required (once regulations published)
2. 12-month grace period for existing operators (~Nov 2026)
3. Full POCAMLA AML/KYC compliance mandatory
4. 10% excise duty collection on service fees
5. Existing operators (Yellow Card, Kotani Pay, Payd) demonstrate commercial viability

**Penalties for non-compliance:**
- Companies: Fines up to KSh 20-25 million
- Individuals: Fines up to KSh 10 million, imprisonment 5-10 years

## 7. Key Legislation Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Nov 2023 | National Treasury directs crypto regulatory framework creation |
